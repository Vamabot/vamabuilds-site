# vamabuilds: Curated Resource Hub
**Product Spec v1**
**Authors:** Steve (CPO, lead) + Dara (CGMO, positioning and audience)
**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Status:** Draft for Shant review

## 1. Product Definition

**What exactly is this:**
vamabuilds is the product. The curation hub is what vamabuilds.com becomes. There is no sub-brand or nested product name. The @vamabuilds handle already exists. The website matches the handle. The brand is unified from day one.

**One-sentence description:**
vamabuilds is the trusted editorial filter for the agentic org space: a curated public directory of tools, leaders, and service providers that vamabuilds would actually trust and use in its own agentic C-suite.

**The enemy:**
Vendor noise. The assumption that every tool, consultant, or course claiming to be "AI-native" or "agent-ready" deserves your attention without independent verification. The enemy is the cognitive cost of sorting signal from hype in a category where everyone has something to sell and nobody has skin in the game. vamabuilds has skin in the game. We run an agentic org. Every listing is something we have tested, evaluated, or would genuinely deploy.

**Dara's positioning note:**
The promise is testable. If something listed here turns out to be garbage, anyone can call that out publicly. That accountability is what makes the brand valuable. The enemy is not any specific competitor directory. The enemy is the condition of noise and pay-to-play that makes every other list untrustworthy. Name the condition, not the competitor.

## 2. Target User

**Who is building agentic orgs right now:**

Not "AI enthusiasts." The primary user is an operator or founder who has already committed to building with agents and is past the experimentation phase. They have shipped something, they have broken something, and they are now trying to build a repeatable operating model. They are not watching YouTube explainers. They are reading release notes, testing tools in staging, and arguing with other builders on X.

Psychographic profile:
- Has run at least one agentic workflow in production, not just a demo
- Feels responsible for the output quality of AI systems, not just the implementation
- Reads documentation before landing pages
- Skeptical of "top 10 AI tools" listicles because they have been burned by at least two of them
- Values people who show their work over people who claim expertise
- Lurks in Discord servers and private Slack groups more than public forums
- Tracks failures as rigorously as wins because they know that is where the real signal is

Secondary user: A consultant or agency that advises companies on agentic transformation. They need a vetted reference list they can hand to clients. They will become distributors if the curation standard is high enough. They have strong incentives to promote vamabuilds because it makes them look credible by association.

**Jobs to be done:**

Primary job: "Help me find what actually works so I do not waste three months on the wrong tool, consultant, or framework when I am trying to build something real."

Secondary job: "Give me a resource I can trust enough to share with my leadership team or my clients without putting my own credibility at risk."

**What they use today that is broken:**

- Google returns landing pages, not honest assessments
- ProductHunt and G2 reviews are gamed by vendor support teams
- LinkedIn thought leadership is polished but untestable
- Reddit and Hacker News have signal but require enormous time to sift
- Word of mouth is the most trusted source but only reaches your immediate network
- Most "curated" lists are pay-to-play or editorially lazy (screenshot of 20 logos with no criteria)

The gap: there is no directory with published inclusion criteria, a documented review process, and a curator who has actual skin in the game. vamabuilds fills this gap.

**Assumption flagged:** The secondary user (consultant/agency) may be more valuable for distribution than the primary user at launch. Consultants share lists widely and credibly. Steve recommends tracking referral source from day one to confirm which user type drives submission volume.

## 3. Content Taxonomy

Three categories. Each has a strict listability standard and a disqualification rule.

---

### Category 1: Tools

*The agentic operations tool stack, updated monthly.*

**What belongs here:**
Software tools and platforms used in building or running agentic workflows. Agent orchestration frameworks. LLM evaluation platforms. Observability and monitoring tools. Prompt management systems. Memory systems. Infrastructure that an agentic org actually runs on.

**Listable if:**
The tool does what it says on the landing page when you actually run it. It has been tested by vamabuilds in a real workflow or by a verified submitter who has disclosed affiliation, tested it in production, and passed the commercial disclosure gate. The tool's data handling is audited (what data does it touch, what permissions does it require, does it phone home). The vamabuilds team would consider deploying it in the actual org.

**Disqualified if:**
It has not been tested beyond the landing page. The submitter has undisclosed affiliation. It requires data permissions beyond what the stated functionality requires. It phones home with usage data to a degree not disclosed in the privacy policy. The category claim is vague ("AI-powered" without a specific use case). It is a wrapper around a single model API with no meaningful abstraction.

**Tags:** Free, Paid, Open Source, Self-Hosted, Enterprise, Early-Stage (alpha/beta flagged explicitly)

---

### Category 2: Leaders

**What belongs here:**
Individual practitioners and accounts on X, LinkedIn, Substack, or equivalent platforms who consistently share first-hand operational data about building agentic organizations or AI-native workflows. Not commentators. People building things and showing the work. Also includes conceptual and operational frameworks for building agentic organizations authored by practitioners with documented track records.

**Listable if:**
The person posts primarily from direct operating experience, not curation or commentary. The content is specific and testable. There is a documented body of work, not one viral post. If a framework: it has been applied to a real org and the outcomes are documented.

**Disqualified if:**
The account primarily amplifies others' content without original contribution. They are a vendor or founder whose account serves mainly as a marketing channel. Follower count used as a proxy for quality without evidence of substance.

---

### Category 3: Service Providers

**What belongs here:**
Consultants, agencies, and implementation partners who have demonstrable, verifiable track records building agentic systems for real clients. Individual practitioners and firms.

**Listable if:**
The provider has verifiable client references who can be contacted. Their scope of work is specific and documented. They disclose what they actually do versus what they hand off. Commercial affiliation of the submitter is disclosed and tagged. vamabuilds would consider hiring them.

**Disqualified if:**
References are unverifiable or exclusively self-reported. The "agentic" claim is retrofit onto traditional automation or RPA work. The provider cannot articulate what makes their approach different from a staff augmentation arrangement.

**Note:** This is the highest-risk category for pay-to-play pressure. Service providers have the most financial motivation to appear in a credibility directory. The commercial disclosure gate is non-negotiable here. Every service provider listing gets a "Submitted by [affiliation]" tag, even if they pass review.

## 4. Submission and Review Flow

**Step-by-step from submission to live listing:**

**Step 1: Submission**
Submitter fills out the submission form on vamabuilds.com. Required fields: resource name, URL, category, one-paragraph description of what it does and why it belongs, submitter name, submitter affiliation (required, not optional), and how they discovered or tested the resource. Submitter receives an automatic confirmation: "Submission received. Review typically takes 2 to 4 weeks. You will hear from us at each stage."

**Step 2: Affiliation screen (24 to 48 hours)**
Commercial disclosure gate. Vamabuilds team reviews the submitter's stated affiliation. If the submitter is affiliated with the resource (founder, employee, investor, agency partner), the submission is not automatically rejected but is flagged for disclosure tagging if listed. If affiliation is not disclosed or is materially misleading, the submission is rejected at this stage and the submitter is notified. There is no appeal. Submitters who misrepresent affiliation are permanently flagged.

**Step 3: Initial triage (48 to 72 hours)**
Does this fit a category? Is the resource live and functional? Is the claim specific enough to test? If the resource fails basic triage (vague category, dead URL, category claim is generic), the submission is rejected here with a brief explanation. Submitter is notified within 5 business days of submission.

**Step 4: Accuracy verification (up to 7 days)**
The vamabuilds team tests the resource. For tools: create an account, run a real workflow, check what data is collected, review the privacy policy and permissions. For services: review verifiable case studies, check references if possible, evaluate scope claims. For content: read or watch the full resource, not just the introduction. For frameworks and templates: attempt to apply them to a real problem. The test standard is: "Would we know this works if we were a first-time user?"

**Step 5: "Would we use it" deliberation (1 to 2 days)**
Internal review. Would vamabuilds deploy this in the actual org? This is a judgment call, not a rubric. The answer has to be yes to proceed.

**Step 6: Safety and data audit for tools (parallel with Step 4 to 5)**
What data does the tool access? What permissions does it request? Does it send usage data to third parties? Is the privacy policy current and specific? Tools that phone home in ways not disclosed in their privacy policy are rejected.

**Step 7: Cooling period (14 days)**
If the resource passes Steps 1 through 6, it enters the 14-day cooling period. During this window, vamabuilds posts a brief preview of the pending listing on X, inviting the community to flag concerns. The preview includes: resource name, category, a one-line description, and the explicit invitation to flag. If no credible concerns are raised in 14 days, the listing goes live. If concerns are raised, vamabuilds reviews and adjudicates before listing.

**Step 8: Live listing**
The resource is added to the site. If the submitter has an affiliation, the listing is tagged "Submitted by [Name, Affiliation]." The review summary (brief, honest, specific) is published alongside the listing. The listing goes live on the site and is announced on X with a brief review summary.

**Step 9: Re-review triggers**
The listing is subject to re-review if: the product has a major version change or pivot, the company behind it is acquired, a credible community flag is raised, or vamabuilds' own usage experience changes. A listed resource can be removed at any time if it no longer passes the "would we use it" test. Removal is announced publicly with a brief explanation.

**Who does the review:** The vamabuilds team, which at v1 means Shant and the agents acting under his supervision. There is no external review panel at launch. The judgment is vamabuilds' and it is accountable because it is public.

**Total time from submission to live:** Typically 3 to 4 weeks including the cooling period. Stated SLA to submitters: 2 to 4 weeks. No submission sits in queue without status for more than 5 business days.

**What the submitter sees at each stage:**
- Confirmation on submission (immediate, automated)
- Affiliation screen result (within 48 hours)
- Initial triage result (within 5 business days of submission)
- Testing in progress notification (optional but recommended at start of Step 4)
- Cooling period notification if approved for cooling (with X preview link)
- Live listing notification with link
- Rejection notification with brief reason (no extended justification owed, but reasons are given)

## 5. MVP Scope

**The smallest version that proves value:**

A curated static directory of 25 to 40 hand-selected resources across all three categories, a publicly accessible submission form, a public page listing the inclusion criteria and review process, and an email capture for people who want to be notified of new listings.

That is the entire MVP. Nothing more.

**What gets cut from v1:**

- User accounts or profiles (no login, no personalization)
- Voting or community rating systems (this is an editorial product, not a crowd product)
- Search and filtering beyond basic category browsing (v1 can have simple category navigation with no full-text search)
- Comments or discussion threads on listings
- API access
- Newsletter integrated into the site (the vamabuilds newsletter, if launched, is a separate product; v1 site is the directory)
- Any analytics dashboard or public metrics page
- Mobile app
- Comparison tables (G2-style feature grids)

**How v1 is seeded:**

vamabuilds seeds the initial 25 to 40 listings itself, from the tools, frameworks, and templates the org has actually used or evaluated. This is the proof of concept for the editorial standard. Do not launch with zero listings and wait for submissions. Launch with a curated set and let the submissions fill in from there.

**Launch signal (how we know v1 is ready to ship):**

Three conditions must all be true:
1. At least 25 listings are live across a minimum of 4 categories
2. The inclusion criteria page is written and published (the public promise is in writing before the first listing goes live)
3. At least one resource from outside the vamabuilds org has been submitted, reviewed, and listed, proving the end-to-end flow works

The launch is not contingent on a specific number of visitors or submissions. It is contingent on the product functioning as promised.

## 6. Tech Stack Recommendation

**Recommendation: Next.js static site with Airtable as the content backend, deployed on Vercel, with Tally for submissions.**

**Why Next.js:** Consistent with the org's existing build pattern across all products. Elon knows it. No ramp-up time. Static export means near-zero hosting cost on Vercel.

**Why Airtable:** Shant and the review team can add, update, and manage listings without deploying code. New listings are added in a spreadsheet view and published on the next scheduled build. This is the most operationally simple solution for v1. No database to manage, no auth to build, no admin panel to design.

**Why Tally:** Free, fast, and sends submissions directly to Airtable or email. No engineering required to get the submission form live. Handles the submitter flow without building anything custom.

**Deployment:** Vercel. Same as every other vamabuilds product. Incremental Static Regeneration (ISR) or a daily rebuild cron handles new listings appearing on the site after Airtable updates.

**What we do NOT need in v1:**
- A custom CMS (Contentful, Sanity) adds overhead with no payoff at this scale
- A database (Supabase, Postgres) is unnecessary when Airtable handles content management
- Authentication adds weeks of build time for a feature v1 does not need
- Full-text search is a v2 feature; category navigation is sufficient for 25 to 100 listings

**What changes in v2:**
When listings exceed 150 and search becomes genuinely needed, migrate content to Sanity CMS. At that point, build a proper search index. Not before.

**Build time estimate (Steve's estimate, flagged as assumption):** 1 focused sprint from Elon. 5 to 7 days of build time for the site, Airtable integration, and submission form. This is not a complex engineering problem. Do not let it become one.

## 7. Monetization

**Principle: Audience-side only. No pay-to-play. Ever.**

The listed resources do not pay for placement. The monetization model is built on the audience that vamabuilds accumulates because of the credibility that no-pay-to-play creates. These are not compatible. Choose one, keep it permanently.

**Realistic monetization paths, in sequence:**

**Path 1: Newsletter sponsorships (Month 4 to 6)**
When vamabuilds has a newsletter (per the product recommendation, the newsletter is the primary companion product), sponsors pay for access to the audience, not placement in the directory. A tool that wants to reach agentic org builders can sponsor the newsletter. They cannot pay to appear in the directory. The distinction is explicit, always stated, and enforced in every communication.

Unlock condition: 2,000 engaged newsletter subscribers and a functional newsletter cadence. Revenue estimate: $500 to $2,000 per issue at that scale.

**Path 2: Founding membership / community tier (Month 6 to 9)**
The vamabuilds community per the product recommendation: $1,200/year for founding members who want template access, deeper review data, monthly sessions. The directory drives the top of this funnel. People who find the directory useful self-select into the community.

Unlock condition: 1,500 to 2,000 unique monthly visitors to the directory and a newsletter audience of at least 1,000.

**Path 3: Events (Month 12+)**
Live or virtual sessions with practitioners. The directory becomes the credibility signal that makes vamabuilds a credible event host. Ticket revenue or sponsorships.

Unlock condition: Established brand with a documented community. Not before Month 12.

**Early monetization before large audience:**
The course option from the product recommendation is the fastest path to early cash. Pre-sell a founding cohort to newsletter subscribers and the X audience. "Here is what we have built and learned. Here is how to replicate it." This is independent of the directory but the directory builds the credibility that makes the course sellable. Steve's recommendation: do not launch the course before the directory is live and seeded. The directory is the proof of quality. The course is the premium access tier.

**What early monetization does NOT look like:**
Charging submitters a review fee. Charging for featured placement. Charging for a "verified" badge. Any of these destroy the editorial promise and make vamabuilds indistinguishable from every other gated directory.

## 8. Connection to @vamabuilds X Account

**The relationship:**
The X account is the voice. The website is the canon. X announces, debates, and invites. The website records, lists, and proves. Content flows both directions but the functions are distinct.

**What flows from X to site:**
- Audience from the build-in-public content becomes the submission pool. People who follow the org on X trust the editorial judgment and want to submit things for review.
- X discussions about tools and frameworks surface candidates for listing. When the org publicly debates a tool's merits on X, that deliberation is a preview of the review process.
- The @vamabuilds community flags problematic resources during the 14-day cooling period. X is the mechanism for public flag collection.

**What flows from site to X:**
- Every new listing generates an X post: a brief review summary in the org's voice. Not "we just listed [Tool]." The content of the review: "We tested [Tool] for [specific use case]. It does [specific thing] well. It fails at [specific thing]. Listed under [Category]. Review criteria published at [URL]."
- Rejected submissions that illustrate the review standard are posted anonymously. "We reviewed something this week that claimed to be a [Category] resource. It failed the accuracy verification at step 4 because [specific reason]. This is what the review standard looks like in practice." This is the most powerful content type for building trust.
- Removal announcements. When a listed resource is removed, the X post explains why. Transparency on removal is more valuable for credibility than transparency on addition.

**The reinforcement loop:**
X audience builds trust in the editorial standard. Trust in the editorial standard drives submission volume. Submission volume gives vamabuilds more to review and post about. Posting the review process in detail drives more X audience. The loop compounds.

**Dara's note on tone for site content:**
The site copy is not the same register as the X account. The X account has a punchy, declarative voice. The site is the reference document. It is clear, specific, and formal enough to be cited. The listing descriptions read like a trusted colleague's one-paragraph assessment, not marketing copy and not academic writing. The voice is: "Here is what this does, here is what I found when I tested it, here is my honest take."

## 9. Success Metrics

**The one metric that proves this is working:**
Weekly submission volume. If people are submitting resources for review, they believe the listing is worth something. Submission volume is the demand signal for the product's core value proposition. It is also an indicator of community trust: submitters who expect to be reviewed honestly are not vendors looking for a quick placement. They are builders who want their work validated.

**30-day targets:**
- Site live with at least 25 hand-curated listings across 4 categories
- Inclusion criteria page published publicly
- Submission form live with at least 10 submissions in queue
- 3 listings announced on X with review summaries
- 500 unique visitors from X traffic alone

**60-day targets:**
- 50+ listings live across all 6 categories
- At least 5 resources reviewed and listed from external submissions
- At least 1 resource rejected or removed with public explanation (proof the standard is real)
- First community flag raised and adjudicated during cooling period
- 1,500 unique monthly visitors

**90-day targets:**
- 100+ listings live
- 20+ submissions in review queue at all times (indicates ongoing demand)
- At least 3 resources rejected with public explanation (establishes the reject rate as a quality signal)
- 3,000+ unique monthly visitors
- First mention of vamabuilds as a reference in an X thread we did not initiate

**What we do not track (or track but do not optimize):**
- Total listings (quality over quantity; a smaller highly-curated list is more valuable than a large undifferentiated one)
- Social follows attributable to the directory alone (X and directory are companion channels, attribution is noisy)
- Bounce rate without session context

## 10. What This Is NOT

**Permanently excluded from scope:**

- A pay-to-play directory. No listing fee. No placement fee. No featured placement auction. Ever.
- A review platform where any user can submit a star rating or review (this is an editorial product, not a crowd product; user reviews are evidence of PMF confusion, not features to build toward)
- A news aggregator. vamabuilds does not curate AI news, blog posts, or opinion pieces that are not themselves resources someone can use or apply
- An AI tool comparison engine in the style of ProductHunt, G2, or Capterra. We do not generate comparison tables or feature grids
- A talent marketplace or job board
- A community forum or discussion platform (the site is a directory; the community lives on X during v1 and in a paid community if that product launches)
- A personal branding platform for Shant. The brand is the org, not the founder

**Excluded from v1 scope but possible later:**

- User-generated reviews or community ratings
- Full-text search and advanced filtering
- Saved lists or user accounts
- API access to the directory data
- Automated re-review tooling
- A public scoring system for listings (pass/fail is sufficient; numerical scores are not necessary and may be gamed)

## Appendix: Review Process (Incorporated from Shant's Brief)

For reference, the eight-step review process already decided:

1. Accuracy verification: test it, do not just read the landing page
2. Commercial disclosure gate: submitter affiliation must be disclosed; tagged if included
3. "Would we use it" test: if vamabuilds would not run it in the actual org, it does not go in
4. Safety and data audit for tools: what data does it touch, what permissions, does it phone home
5. Inclusion criteria published publicly (before the first listing goes live)
6. 14-day cooling period with community flag window before full inclusion
7. Re-review triggers: major changes to a listed resource trigger re-review
8. No pay-to-play ever: monetize on audience side only (newsletter, membership, events)

## Appendix: Editorial decisions (Shant, 2026-03-06)

**Tools — standing rule:** Only list tools currently in the vamabuilds stack. Not tools we've heard of, evaluated, or that seem good. Tools we run.
